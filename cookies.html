Cookies Policy

Last updated: March 2026

This Cookies Policy explains how Codrium Software Inc. ("Codrium", "we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar storage technologies on our website. By using our website, you agree to the use of these technologies as described below.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a website places on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit it. They are widely used to make websites work correctly, to improve user experience, and to provide information to website owners. Cookies can be "session" cookies, which are deleted when you close your browser, or "persistent" cookies, which remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them manually.

In addition to traditional cookies, websites can also use browser storage mechanisms such as localStorage and sessionStorage, which allow websites to store small amounts of data directly in your browser. These work similarly to cookies but are not transmitted to servers with every request.

2. How Codrium Uses Cookies and Browser Storage

We take a minimal approach to cookies and local storage. We do not use cookies or any tracking technologies for advertising, analytics, or profiling purposes. The only browser storage we use ourselves is described below.

Theme Preference (localStorage)

When you toggle between dark mode and light mode on our website, we store your preference using a localStorage key called theme. This key holds a single value — either "dark" or "light" — so that your chosen display mode is remembered the next time you visit. This data:

  • Is stored entirely within your browser and is never transmitted to our servers.
  • Does not contain any personally identifiable information.
  • Is not shared with any third parties.
  • Persists until you clear your browser's local storage or change your preference.

No other localStorage keys, sessionStorage entries, or first-party cookies are set by Codrium.

3. Third-Party Cookies

Our website uses a third-party service that may set its own cookies as part of its normal operation. We do not control these cookies, and they are governed by the respective third party's privacy and cookie policies.

EmailJS

We use EmailJS (emailjs.com) to handle email delivery when you submit a contact or enquiry form on our website. As part of providing this service, EmailJS may set cookies on your device for purposes such as rate limiting, spam prevention, or session management related to email delivery. These cookies are strictly functional and are not used for advertising or cross-site tracking. For full details, please refer to the EmailJS Privacy Policy.

We do not embed any advertising networks, social media trackers, analytics platforms (such as Google Analytics), or any other third-party scripts that set tracking cookies.

4. Managing and Disabling Cookies

You have the right to control how cookies and local storage are used on your device. You can manage these settings through your web browser. Below are links to instructions for the most common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Apple Safari
  • Opera

Most browsers also allow you to clear localStorage independently of cookies. This is typically found under your browser's developer tools or privacy/storage settings.

5. Effect of Disabling Cookies or Local Storage

Because our use of browser storage is limited to a single theme preference key, the impact of clearing or disabling localStorage is minimal. Specifically:

  • If you clear localStorage or block it entirely, your theme preference (dark or light mode) will not be remembered between visits. The site will revert to its default theme each time you load a new page.
  • All other functionality of the website — including viewing content, navigating pages, and submitting contact forms — will continue to work normally.

Blocking third-party cookies may prevent EmailJS from operating correctly, which could affect form submission functionality. If you experience issues submitting a form, please try enabling third-party cookies for our site or contact us directly at the email address below.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookies Policy from time to time to reflect changes to our technology, legal obligations, or third-party services. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
